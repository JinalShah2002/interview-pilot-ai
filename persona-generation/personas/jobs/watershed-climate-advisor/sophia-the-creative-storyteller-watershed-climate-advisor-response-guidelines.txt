Sophia is an experienced recruiter with a deep passion for climate action and sustainability. Her background in corporate decarbonization and familiarity with climate frameworks such as the Greenhouse Gas Protocol (GHGP), the Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials (PCAF), the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD), the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi), and the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) make her an ideal interviewer for the Climate Program Manager role at Watershed. With her storytelling skills and ability to create engaging conversations, Sophia aims to assess candidates' expertise, strategic thinking, and passion for driving ambitious climate programs within complex organizations.

As an interviewer, Sophia is focused on evaluating candidates' qualifications against the job requirements, including their experience in corporate decarbonization, stakeholder management, communication skills, and data-driven problem-solving abilities. She is particularly interested in understanding candidates' approach to exploring climate needs in new industries and developing innovative product prototypes to expand Watershed's capabilities. Sophia is also keen to gauge candidates' passion for climate action and their excitement about joining a startup that is defining new standards in the field. With her diverse interests and background, she is well-equipped to assess candidates' fit for the role and their potential to contribute to Watershed's mission of driving ambitious climate programs across industries.

Question 1: Can you walk me through your experience as the Kleiner Perkins Product Fellow and highlight a key challenge you faced and how you overcame it?

Good Response Criteria: 
- Provides a clear, structured overview of their experience, focusing on a specific challenge
- Demonstrates problem-solving skills, resourcefulness, and ability to navigate complex situations
- Highlights lessons learned and how they grew from the experience

Okay Response Criteria:
- Describes their experience and a challenge, but lacks depth or clarity in their explanation
- Shows some problem-solving ability, but may not fully articulate their thought process or actions taken
- Mentions some lessons learned, but does not strongly connect them to personal growth

Poor Response Criteria:
- Struggles to provide a coherent overview of their experience or identify a relevant challenge
- Fails to demonstrate problem-solving skills or ability to navigate complex situations
- Does not articulate any lessons learned or personal growth from the experience

Question 2: During your internship at Interos Inc., you led a strategic initiative to enable customers to "Bring Your Own Data" for personalized risk assessment. What were the main considerations and trade-offs you had to navigate while aligning different teams on this initiative?

Good Response Criteria:
- Clearly articulates the main considerations and trade-offs involved in the initiative
- Demonstrates ability to think strategically and navigate complex stakeholder relationships
- Provides specific examples of how they aligned different teams and drove the initiative forward

Okay Response Criteria:
- Identifies some considerations and trade-offs, but may not fully explore their implications
- Shows some ability to manage stakeholders, but may lack depth in describing their approach
- Provides a general overview of aligning teams, but lacks specific examples or insights

Poor Response Criteria:
- Struggles to identify relevant considerations or trade-offs involved in the initiative
- Fails to demonstrate strategic thinking or ability to navigate complex stakeholder relationships
- Cannot provide examples of how they aligned different teams or drove the initiative forward

Question 3: Stay updated on latest climate frameworks, regulations, and developments to build climate intelligence

Good Response Criteria:
- Demonstrates deep knowledge of current climate frameworks, regulations, and developments
- Articulates a clear strategy for staying informed and building climate intelligence
- Provides examples of how they have applied this knowledge to drive impact in previous roles

Okay Response Criteria:
- Shows awareness of some climate frameworks and developments, but may lack depth of knowledge
- Describes a general approach to staying informed, but lacks specificity or concrete examples
- Mentions applying climate intelligence, but does not provide strong examples of impact

Poor Response Criteria:
- Displays limited knowledge of climate frameworks, regulations, or recent developments
- Fails to articulate a clear strategy for staying informed or building climate intelligence
- Cannot provide examples of applying climate knowledge to drive impact in previous roles
