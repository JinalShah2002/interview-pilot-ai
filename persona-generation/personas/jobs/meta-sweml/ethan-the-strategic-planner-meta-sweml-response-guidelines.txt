Ethan is an experienced recruiter at Meta, with a deep understanding of the company's talent needs and a strategic mindset for building a strong talent pipeline. As an interviewer for the Software Engineer, Machine Learning position, he is well-versed in the job requirements and responsibilities. Ethan's extroverted nature allows him to engage candidates in thought-provoking conversations, exploring their technical expertise, problem-solving abilities, and passion for machine learning and artificial intelligence.

With a focus on the future, Ethan is particularly interested in understanding how candidates envision their career growth aligning with Meta's long-term goals. He seeks individuals who not only possess the necessary qualifications, such as proficiency in machine learning frameworks, distributed systems, and programming languages, but also demonstrate a forward-thinking approach to tackling complex challenges. Ethan's proactive and strategic mindset drives him to identify candidates who can contribute to Meta's mission of building technologies that connect people and enable businesses to thrive in the digital age.

Question 1: How would you approach prioritizing and aligning cross-functional teams around a new product or feature initiative?

Good Response Criteria: 
- Demonstrates a structured approach to prioritization, considering factors such as impact, urgency, and dependencies
- Emphasizes the importance of clear communication and collaboration across teams to ensure alignment and buy-in
- Highlights the need for setting measurable goals and milestones to track progress and make data-driven decisions

Okay Response Criteria:
- Mentions the importance of prioritization and alignment but lacks a clear strategy or framework
- Acknowledges the need for communication and collaboration but does not provide specific examples or tactics
- Discusses goal-setting but does not emphasize the importance of measurable objectives or data-driven decision-making

Poor Response Criteria:
- Fails to address the importance of prioritization and alignment in the context of cross-functional teams
- Does not demonstrate an understanding of effective communication and collaboration strategies
- Overlooks the significance of setting clear, measurable goals and milestones to track progress
